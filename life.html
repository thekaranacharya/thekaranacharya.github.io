Life

You're not your job. You're not how much money you have in the bank.
You're not the car you drive. You're not the contents of your wallet.
You're not your f*king khakis. You're the all-singing, all-dancing crap of the world.

- Tyler Durden, Fight Club (1999)

Everything is made up of strings, even us mortal humans, according to String Theory. We're like balloons.
All balloons have the same constituent inside : air. So how do we tell one from the other? They just come in different sizes and colors.
It's exactly like that with us.
We try to advertise (sell) ourselves on our social media platforms, making the best quips and posting the wittiest comments.
We try to be unique. We gamble. For attention & validation.
But aren't we forgetting we're all basically just a beautiful, unique collection of interlinked cells (and strings within) trying to live on oxygen?

The needs of a happy life are few. The best things in life are free.

There's more to life than work.


My Leisure Interests

Films & TV

♥️ getting lost in the world of films and television shows which are essentially wormholes that transport us to new dimensions of space and time.

Following are some of my favorite films & tv shows :

Interstellar


A team of explorers travel through a wormhole in space in an attempt to ensure humanity's survival.

Directed by

Christopher Nolan

Breaking Bad


A high school chemistry teacher diagnosed with inoperable lung cancer turns to manufacturing and selling methamphetamine in order to secure his family's future.

Created by

Vince Gilligan

The Social Network


As Harvard student Mark Zuckerberg creates the social networking site that would become known as Facebook, he is sued by the twins who claimed he stole their idea, and by the co-founder who was later squeezed out of the business.

Directed by

David Fincher

Black Mirror


An anthology series exploring a twisted, high-tech multiverse where humanity's greatest innovations and darkest instincts collide.

Created by

Charlie Brooker

The Dark Knight


When the menace known as the Joker wreaks havoc and chaos on the people of Gotham, Batman must accept one of the greatest psychological and physical tests of his ability to fight injustice.

Directed by

Christopher Nolan

Mr. Robot


Elliot, a brilliant but highly unstable young cyber-security engineer and vigilante hacker, becomes a key figure in a complex game of global dominance when he and his shadowy allies try to take down the corrupt corporation he works for.

Created by

Sam Esmail

Arrival


A linguist works with the military to communicate with alien lifeforms after twelve mysterious spacecraft appear around the world.

Directed by

Denis Villenueve

Mad Men


A drama about one of New York's most prestigious ad agencies at the beginning of the 1960s, focusing on one of the firm's most mysterious but extremely talented ad executives, Donald Draper.

Created by

Matthew Weiner
